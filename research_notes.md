## Research Notes

### Wikipedia Summary
Hurricanes are rapidly rotating tropical cyclones with low-pressure centers, strong winds, and heavy rain. They form over warm ocean waters, drawing energy from evaporation and condensation, and can cause severe coastal impacts such as storm surge, flooding, and tornadoes. Climate change is increasing their intensity, duration, and rainfall potential, affecting regions like Florida.

### NOAA Hurricane Preparedness Guidelines (key points)
- **Stay Informed**: Monitor local forecasts, alerts, and the National Hurricane Center updates.
- **Create a Family Emergency Plan**: Identify evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication methods.
- **Build an Emergency Kit**: Include water, non‑perishable food, medications, battery‑powered radios, and important documents.
- **Secure Your Home**: Install storm shutters, reinforce doors, and clear gutters to reduce flood risk.
- **Know Your Community Resources**: Sign up as a Storm Spotter or Cooperative Observer, and be aware of local shelters and public warning systems.
- **Follow Official Instructions**: Heed evacuation orders, stay indoors during storm surge warnings, and avoid flooded roads.
- **Utilize NOAA Tools**: Use NOAA’s weather maps, radar, and the Weather‑Ready Nation resources for ongoing preparedness and education.
